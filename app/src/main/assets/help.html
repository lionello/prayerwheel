Spin the wheel clockwise: the mantras are written in the direction of the movement of the sun across the sky.

A prayer wheel is a cylindrical 'wheel' on a spindle. The mantra Om Mani Padme Hum is written in Sanskrit on the wheel. According to the Tibetan Buddhist tradition, spinning such a wheel will have the same effect as reciting the prayers.

Prayer wheels are used to accumulate wisdom and merit (good karma) and to purify negativities (bad karma). As you turn the wheel, it is best to focus the mind and repeat the Om Mani Padme Hum mantra. Not only does this increase the merit earned by the wheel's use, but it is a mind stabilization technique that trains the mind while the body is in motion. However, it is said that even turning it while distracted has benefits and merits.

It's best to turn the wheel with a gentle rhythm and not too fast or frantically. While turning smoothly keep in mind the motivation and spirit of compassion and bodhichitta (the noble mind that aspires to full enlightenment for the benefit of all beings).

At the end of a practice session there's a Tibetan Buddhist tradition of dedicating any accumulated merits that you may have gathered during practice to the benefit of all sentient beings. Then Om Ah Hum 3 times.

His Holiness, the Dalai Lama, has said that having the mantra on your computer works the same as a traditional Mani wheel. As the image turns, waves of compassion emanate in all directions to the surrounding area.
